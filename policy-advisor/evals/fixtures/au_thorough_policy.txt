Wattle & Co Financial Services Pty Ltd — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 March 2026

1. About this policy
This privacy policy explains how Wattle & Co Financial Services Pty Ltd ("Wattle & Co",
"we") manages personal information. It is available free of charge on our website and in
print on request, and we review and update it regularly. Questions and complaints about
how we handle personal information can be directed to our Privacy Officer
(privacy@wattleco.example). We acknowledge complaints within 7 days and aim to resolve
them within 30 days; you may also contact the Office of the Australian Information
Commissioner.

2. Anonymity and pseudonyms
Where it is lawful and practicable, you may deal with us anonymously or using a
pseudonym, for example when making a general product enquiry. Identification is required
where the law obliges us to verify your identity, such as under anti-money-laundering
rules.

3. What we collect and why
We only collect personal information that is reasonably necessary for our lending and
financial advisory functions, such as contact details, employment and financial
information. We collect information directly from you by lawful and fair means, through
application forms, our website and phone calls. We only collect sensitive information,
such as health information for insurance applications, with your consent.

4. Unsolicited information
If we receive personal information we did not ask for and determine we could not have
collected it ourselves, we destroy or de-identify it as soon as practicable, where lawful
and reasonable.

5. Collection notices
At or before the time we collect personal information, we notify you of who we are, how
to contact us, the purposes of collection, the kinds of organisations we usually disclose
information to, and how to access, correct or complain about our handling of your
information.

6. How we use and disclose information
We use and disclose personal information for the purposes for which it was collected,
such as assessing applications and servicing your account. We only use it for other
purposes with your consent, where you would reasonably expect a related use, or where
required by law. We disclose information to credit reporting bodies, our regulators and
service providers under contract.

7. Direct marketing
We only send direct marketing about our products where you would reasonably expect it or
have consented. Every marketing message includes a simple unsubscribe mechanism, you can
opt out at any time by contacting us, and we honour opt-out requests promptly.

8. Overseas disclosure
Some of our service providers store data in New Zealand and Ireland. Before disclosing
personal information overseas we take reasonable steps, including contractual clauses,
to ensure overseas recipients handle it consistently with the Australian Privacy
Principles, and we remain accountable for that information.

9. Government identifiers
We do not adopt government related identifiers, such as Medicare or driver licence
numbers, as our own customer identifiers, and we only use or disclose them where
required for identity verification or by law.

10. Data quality
We take reasonable steps to keep the personal information we hold accurate, up to date
and complete, including periodic revalidation of contact details and prompts to update
your information.

11. Security and retention
We protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss and unauthorised access,
modification or disclosure using encryption in transit and at rest, role-based access
controls, staff security training and regular penetration testing. When information is no
longer needed for a permitted purpose and no law requires retention, we destroy or
de-identify it under our retention schedule.

12. Access to your information
You can request access to the personal information we hold about you by contacting the
Privacy Officer. We respond within 30 days, provide access in the manner you request
where reasonable, and give written reasons and complaint options in the rare cases
access is refused.

13. Corrections
If information we hold is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete or misleading, you can ask
us to correct it and we will take reasonable steps to do so, tell other organisations we
gave it to where you ask and it is practicable, and give you written reasons if we
refuse.
